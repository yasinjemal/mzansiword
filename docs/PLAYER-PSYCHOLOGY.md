# PLAYER-PSYCHOLOGY.md

**Owner:** Behavioral Psychologist · **Canonical:** [Bible §3](./GAME-DESIGN-BIBLE.md#3-what-the-best-games-actually-teach-us-principles-not-features)

The transferable value from the best games is **psychological principles, not
features** — most of their features exist to monetize power, which we cannot do.
Each principle below carries evidence ([`RESEARCH-2026.md`](./RESEARCH-2026.md))
and a MzansiWord translation.

## The drives we use (ethically)

- **Loss aversion** — the strongest retention force. People work ~2× harder to
  avoid losing what they have. → the streak is the spine ([`RETENTION.md`](./RETENTION.md)).
- **The 7-day lock-in** — retention "locks in" at a 7-day streak; obsess over
  0→7. Forgiving early (2 shields, free repair), meaningful late.
- **Scarcity creates appointments** — one shared Word of the Day makes each play
  precious and powers the spoiler-free share. Practice lives separately so scarcity
  stays on the marquee.
- **Variable rewards drive anticipation** — dopamine peaks in *anticipation*; vary
  bonus discoveries and celebration intensity. **Bright line:** frame only *genuine*
  near-misses honestly; never engineer fake near-misses or loss-disguised-as-win.
- **Comprehension is retention** — players don't value what they don't understand;
  an 8-word streak explanation lifted Duolingo DAU 10,000+. Teach by playing.
- **Weekly leagues weaponize loss aversion socially** — fear of relegation
  re-engages harder than hope of promotion.
- **Group identity manufactures return** — visible absence lets your school/
  province down ([`SOCIAL-VIRAL.md`](./SOCIAL-VIRAL.md)).
- **Flow beats compulsion** — a healthy 3–5 min session with a clean end beats
  removing stopping points. Long-term retention comes from absorption, not anxiety.

## Dark patterns we refuse to ship (Principle 2)

Even though they demonstrably work: guilt/shame notifications (the crying owl),
artificial near-misses, paywalling the only streak-save, fake urgency, and
removing stopping points to inflate session time. An educational game giving real
prizes to a vulnerable mass market holds a higher bar — and holding it is good
brand strategy with schools, sponsors, and government.

## Duty of care

If motivation design risks harm (compulsive patterns, minors), it fails the
review board regardless of the retention lift. Wellbeing outranks the metric.
