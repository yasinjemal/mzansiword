# SPONSORS.md

**Owner:** Sponsor Consultant + Product Strategist · **Canonical:** [Bible §11](./GAME-DESIGN-BIBLE.md#11-the-sponsor-platform)

This is the primary revenue engine and the reason the game becomes a national
institution. The pitch is unusually strong: **MzansiWord reaches the exact
mass-market, township, youth audience that MTN, Vodacom, Shoprite, Capitec,
Coca-Cola, and FNB spend fortunes trying to reach — in a lightweight, brand-safe,
educational, feel-good context, rewarding them in the airtime/data currency those
brands already give away.**

## The two anchor relationships

1. **A telco (Vodacom, MTN, or — likely more motivated — Telkom) for zero-rating +
   prize underwriting.** Zero-rating removes the data barrier that is SA's real
   adoption ceiling *and* gives the telco a gamified loyalty surface they already
   build (VodaBucks, MTN Win-Win). **This one deal simultaneously solves
   distribution, prize funding, and education-tier funding. It is the single
   highest-leverage partnership in the plan — pursue it first and hardest**
   (Principle: one of the five things that matter, [`PRINCIPLES.md`](./PRINCIPLES.md)).

   **New regulatory lever (2026):** ICASA obliges all SA networks to zero-rate
   qualifying **public-benefit-organisation** content **by 15 January 2027**
   ([`RESEARCH-2026.md`](./RESEARCH-2026.md) §7). If the education tier qualifies,
   zero-rating may become an entitlement to pursue, not only a deal to strike.
   Investigate with counsel; fold into both the telco talk and the education
   roadmap. Tracked in [`DECISION-LOG.md`](./DECISION-LOG.md).

2. **A mass-market retailer/bank (Shoprite, Capitec, Coca-Cola) for branded
   tournaments and prize pools.** Shoprite's Xtra Savings alone has 33.7M members;
   these brands want township reach and pay in exactly our reward currency.

## The sponsor dashboard (a real product)

Self-serve creation of branded tournaments: branding rules (skinned tournament,
sponsored "double coins" moments, branded puzzle packs — **never pay-to-win**),
prize configuration and fulfillment, and analytics — reach, participation,
completion, regional/age/school/province targeting, ROI vs. spend. Gaming is
underpriced attention (<5% of ad spend); the reporting converts the marketing
manager.

## The hard line (restated)

Sponsors may fund prizes, art, zero-rating, and events. Sponsors may **never** sell
gameplay advantage or buy a player a better chance of winning. "Your logo is on the
trophy, not on a cheat code." Protects CPA compliance and game integrity at once.

## Fulfillment & trust

Prize fulfillment must be reliable and auditable (the daily draw's audit trail is
the template). A sponsor whose promised laptop never arrives is a brand disaster
for them and us. Independent verification is a feature we sell, not overhead.

## Status & KPIs

Not started (first telco conversation is Phase 3, business-led). KPIs: signed
partners, zero-rating coverage, prize funding secured, sponsor ROI and renewal rate.
