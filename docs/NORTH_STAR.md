# NORTH_STAR.md — Above Everything Else

**Version 1.0 · 2026-07-06 · The highest document in the system**

Only [`PRINCIPLES.md`](./PRINCIPLES.md) (how we behave) and this file (what we're
for) sit above the [Game Design Bible](./GAME-DESIGN-BIBLE.md). When you are unsure
whether to build a feature, compare it against the four answers below. If it
doesn't move the game toward them, it probably doesn't belong — regardless of how
clever it is.

---

## 1. If MzansiWord disappeared tomorrow, what would players miss most?

**The daily appointment that makes them feel smart in their own language, and the
airtime it can win them.** Not a feature — a *ritual*: three minutes each day where
a person on the cheapest phone, on expensive data, solves a word, keeps a streak
alive, feels clever, and shares it with someone on WhatsApp. For many players it is
the one game that is *theirs* — South African, in isiXhosa and English, free, and
occasionally life-affecting when the airtime lands. What they'd miss is **belonging
+ competence + a fair shot**, delivered daily.

## 2. What can only MzansiWord offer that no competitor can?

**Real airtime prizes to a mass market, structurally *without* selling anyone an
advantage — in South Africa's own languages, on the cheapest device, through
WhatsApp.** The moat is the stack no rival can copy at once:
- The **CPA-s36 constraint** makes us legally incapable of the pay-to-win model
  global word games run on ([`PRINCIPLES.md`](./PRINCIPLES.md) Prime Directive).
- **isiXhosa/African-language depth** and SA identity (province, school, Ubuntu)
  are un-copyable by Wordscapes or Wordle ([`WORD-SYSTEM.md`](./WORD-SYSTEM.md)).
- **Measurable literacy** in a country with an 81%-can't-read-for-meaning crisis
  ([`EDUCATION.md`](./EDUCATION.md)) — a claim no casual game can credibly make.
- **Airtime/data as the reward** — exactly what this market wants and what sponsors
  already pay in ([`SPONSORS.md`](./SPONSORS.md)).

Any competitor can copy one of these. None can copy the *combination*.

## 3. What feeling should every player have after a 10-minute session?

> **"I'm sharper than I was, I represented something bigger than me, and I can't
> wait for tomorrow."**

Concretely: at least one *named emotion* per session ([`PRINCIPLES.md`](./PRINCIPLES.md)
Principle 6) — "I discovered a word," "I almost got it," "I beat my school," "I won
data," "I kept my streak." Absorbed, not anxious (flow, not compulsion). Ending on
a clean high with the next hook already planted — never nagged, never drained.

## 4. Five years from now, what do we want MzansiWord to be known for?

**The game that helped South Africa read — and became a national institution
without ever selling power.** The things we'd want said:
- *"Schools recommend it, parents trust it, teachers use it."*
- *"It's how my province / my school competes."*
- *"It's the fairest game with real prizes — you win by being good, not by paying."*
- *"It started in isiXhosa and English and now speaks the whole continent."*
- *"It proved you can build a beloved, profitable game by doing the honest thing."*

The five-year test isn't revenue or DAU alone — it's whether MzansiWord is the rare
case where **the ethical thing and the commercially smart thing were the same
thing**, at national scale ([Bible §20](./GAME-DESIGN-BIBLE.md#20-the-one-paragraph-thesis)).

## How to use this file

Before committing to any feature, RFC ([`RFC/`](./RFC/)), or roadmap change, ask:
*which of the four answers does this move us toward, and by how much?* A feature
that serves none is a candidate for the cut list ([`DECISION-LOG.md`](./DECISION-LOG.md)),
no matter how fun it sounds in the room. This is the final quality gate above all
the others in [`PRINCIPLES.md`](./PRINCIPLES.md).
