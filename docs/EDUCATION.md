# EDUCATION.md

**Owner:** Learning Scientist + Education Consultant · **Canonical:** [Bible §12](./GAME-DESIGN-BIBLE.md#12-the-education-platform)

Education is the moral mission, the government/CSR funding case, and a durable B2B
revenue line — *if* built on the right model. The wrong model (charging students,
gating learning behind a paywall) is both legally adjacent-to-trouble under the
promotional-competition rules and ethically indefensible for a literacy product.

## Why now

**PIRLS 2021: 81% of Grade 4 learners cannot read for meaning in any language**
(914,000 children); SA ranked last of 43 countries with the largest decline
([`RESEARCH-2026.md`](./RESEARCH-2026.md)). Government, schools, and CSR funders are
actively looking for interventions. The 2024 BELA Act pushes multilingual and
African-language education. An isiXhosa + English word game is culturally and
politically on-trend.

## The correct model (Prodigy / Siyavula / Eneza pattern)

The **learning core is always free** (Principle 7); revenue comes from
**teacher/admin tooling (B2B)** and **family cosmetics (B2C)** — paying never buys
a grade or an advantage. Siyavula reaches ~100% of SA government schools funded by
CSR/grants; Eneza reaches 10M+ learners via telco partnership. Proven African path,
perfectly aligned with our legal constraint.

## What schools get (free core)

- Curriculum-aligned vocabulary/reading content in home languages, framed against
  CAPS and the reading-for-meaning crisis.
- Teacher dashboard: class rankings, assignment setting, per-learner
  mastery/reading-level analytics, certificates.
- School and class leaderboards; inter-school competitions (on the tournament
  platform, [`TOURNAMENTS.md`](./TOURNAMENTS.md)).
- Learner identity that lets a child "represent their school" — the hook that
  spreads adoption learner-to-learner.

## What generates revenue

- **Optional paid teacher/admin analytics tier** (per-school/district licence) —
  never charges learners (Kahoot/Blooket model).
- **CSR/grant funding** for the free tier, pitched as a *measurable* literacy
  intervention backed by the mastery data the game already collects.
- **Telco-sponsored zero-rated education access** — the same telco relationship
  ([`SPONSORS.md`](./SPONSORS.md)); note the 2027 PBO zero-rating obligation.

## Why this is defensible — measurement is the moat

A word game that can *measure* a learner's vocabulary and reading level improving
over time, in their home language, on the cheapest phone, for free, at national
scale, during a documented reading crisis, is genuinely compelling to the DBE and
every education funder. Build mastery tracking ([`PROGRESSION.md`](./PROGRESSION.md))
rigorously enough to stand behind a funding claim.

## Critical caution (gating)

Do **not** over-promise curriculum integration before the isiXhosa/African-language
content is deep enough to deliver it. Thin content here doesn't just weaken a
feature — it damages credibility with the institutions we most need
([`WORD-SYSTEM.md`](./WORD-SYSTEM.md), Principle 9).

## POPIA

Under-18 learners require provable guardian consent, minor-safe defaults, and data
minimization — built in from day one ([`SECURITY-LEGAL.md`](./SECURITY-LEGAL.md)).

## KPIs

Words learned, reading-level lift (funding-grade), schools onboarded, teacher-tier
conversion, learner D30 in school cohorts.
